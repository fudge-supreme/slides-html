Present Simple — What is it?

We use the Present Simple to describe habits, routines, and facts — things that happen regularly or are always true.

📌 When do we use it?
✅ Daily routines  ·  ✅ Jobs and duties  ·  ✅ Facts about people or things
"She cleans the facial bed every morning."  (routine)
"He works at a beauty clinic."  (job/fact)
"The cream smells like lavender."  (fact about a thing)

✅ Affirmative

SubjectVerb formExample
I / You / We / Theybase verbI clean the room.
He / She / Itverb + s/esShe cleans the room.

⚠️ The –s/es Rule

RuleExample
Most verbs → +sclean → cleans
-ch,-sh,-x,-o → +eswash → washes
consonant+y → iesapply → applies
have → hasShe has a client.
Stance 1 · Slide 2 of 4

Negatives & Questions

❌ Negative
Subject + does not (doesn't) + verb
"She doesn't use harsh products."
"He doesn't work on Sundays."
"It doesn't take long."
❓ Question
Does + he/she/it + verb?
"Does she apply toner?" — "Yes, she does."
"Does he clean the cabin?" — "No, he doesn't."
"What does she use?"

📋 Full Pattern Summary

TypeStructureExample (she)
✅ AffirmativeShe + verbsShe applies the mask.
❌ NegativeShe + doesn't + verbShe doesn't apply the mask.
❓ QuestionDoes she + verb?Does she apply the mask?
💬 Short answerYes/No + pronoun + does/doesn'tYes, she does. / No, she doesn't.

This · That · These · Those + Here · There

We use demonstratives to point to objects. The choice depends on distance (near or far) and number (one or many).

⚡ Quick Rule
THIS = near + 1 thing  |  THESE = near + many things
THAT = far + 1 thing  |  THOSE = far + many things

📍 Near (close to you)

WordExample
This (singular)This cream is for dry skin.
These (plural)These tools are mine.
+ HEREPut the toner here.

🔭 Far (away from you)

WordExample
That (singular)That client is waiting.
Those (plural)Those products are expensive.
+ THEREThe mask is over there.

Present Continuous — Right Now!

We use the Present Continuous for actions happening at this moment. Different from Present Simple (habits), this is about NOW.

✅ Affirmative Formula
Subject + am / is / are + verb–ing
"She is applying the cream."
"He is washing his hands."
"They are studying together."
SubjectBe verb
Iam
He / She / Itis
You / We / Theyare
❌ Negative
Subject + is not (isn't) / are not (aren't) + verb–ing
"She isn't applying the mask."  |  "They aren't cleaning the room."
❓ Question
Is / Are + subject + verb–ing?
"Is she mixing the mask?" — "Yes, she is." / "No, she isn't."
"What is he doing?" — "He is preparing the cabin."

How to Make the –ing Form

Most verbs just add –ing, but some have special rules. Let's practice with cabin vocabulary!

RuleWhen?ExampleResult
Just add –ingMost verbsclean · apply · rinsecleaning · applying · rinsing
Drop silent –eVerb ends in silent –emassage · use · preparemassaging · using · preparing
Double consonantShort verb, CVC patternrub · pat · sitrubbing · patting · sitting
ie → yVerb ends in –ielie · tie · dielying · tying · dying

Stance 3 · Slide 3 of 4

Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Understanding the difference is key! Compare these pairs.

Present Simple (habit/routine)Present Continuous (right now)
She applies cream every day. ✅She is applying cream right now. 🔄
He washes the tools after work. ✅He is washing the tools now. 🔄
The client relaxes during facials. ✅The client is relaxing on the bed. 🔄
She cleans the mirror in the morning. ✅She is cleaning the mirror. 🔄
He teaches on Tuesdays. ✅He is teaching right now. 🔄
⏰ Time clues — Present Simple
every day · always · usually · often · never · on Mondays · in the morning
⚡ Time clues — Present Continuous
right now · at this moment · look! · listen! · currently · today · at the moment
