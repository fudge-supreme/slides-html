Language Focus 2 — Present Tenses

Present Tenses for Instructions & Questions

Present Simple

Subject + base verb

Routines, habits, facts, instructions

✦ "I cleanse the face first."
✦ "Toner balances skin pH."
✦ "Do you use SPF daily?"
✦ "We always patch test first."

Present Continuous

am/is/are + verb-ing

Ongoing actions happening RIGHT NOW

✦ "I am applying serum now."
✦ "It is working into pores."
✦ "Are you feeling okay?"
✦ "I am using a glycolic toner."

Present Perfect

have/has + past participle

Recent actions, experience questions

✦ "I have cleansed your skin."
✦ "Have you ever had a peel?"
✦ "I have applied the serum."
✦ "We have completed extraction."

Pro Tip: During a facial, facialists constantly switch between tenses — present simple to explain steps, present continuous to narrate ongoing actions, and present perfect to summarise what's been done.

Language Focus 3 — Language Functions

Functions of Language in the Spa

A language function is the communicative purpose behind what we say. The same grammar can serve many different functions.

Making Polite Requests

"Could you please lie back for me?"
"Would you mind removing your glasses?"
"May I start with the cleansing step?"
Structure: Could / Would you mind / May I + verb

Giving Warm Instructions

"Please keep your eyes closed during this step."
"Try to breathe deeply and relax."
"Let me know if you feel any discomfort."
Please + imperative / Try to / Let me know if…

Making Suggestions

"I'd recommend trying our hydrating mask."
"You might want to avoid exfoliating at home."
"How about booking a follow-up in 4 weeks?"
I'd recommend / You might want to / How about + -ing

Checking Comfort

"Are you comfortable with the pressure?"
"Is the steam temperature okay for you?"
"How does that feel?"
Are you / Is it + adjective + for you?

Explaining & Informing

"This serum contains hyaluronic acid, which draws moisture to the skin."
"We use steam to open the pores before extraction."
This [product] contains / We use [tool] to + infinitive

Reassuring the Client

"That's completely normal — your skin may feel a little warm."
"Don't worry, the tingling means it's working."
"You're doing great — just a few more minutes."
That's normal / Don't worry + explanation

Stage E — The 10-Step Facial Protocol (Part 1)

Steps 1–5: Cleanse to Extract

1

DOUBLE CLEANSE — Remove makeup & impurities

First with oil-based cleanser, then with a foaming water-based one.

"I'm going to start by removing your makeup with a micellar water first."
"Now I'm applying a foaming cleanser to deep-clean the pores."
2

TONE — Balance the skin's pH

Apply toner to restore the acid mantle after cleansing.

"I'm toning your skin now to rebalance its pH. You might feel a slight cooling sensation."
3

EXFOLIATE — Remove dead skin cells

Chemical or physical exfoliant to brighten and refine texture.

"I'm applying a mild AHA exfoliant. It may tingle slightly — that's completely normal."
"I'll leave it on for two minutes, then rinse it off."
4

STEAM — Open pores with vapour

Steamer positioned 30–40 cm from the face for 5–10 minutes.

"I'm bringing in the steamer now. It will help open your pores for the extraction step."
"Is the steam comfortable? Let me know if it's too warm."
5

EXTRACT — Remove comedones

Carefully extract blackheads and whiteheads when indicated.

"I'm doing a few gentle extractions around the nose. There may be slight pressure — breathe through it."
"I've finished the extractions. Your pores are looking much clearer now."
Stage E — The 10-Step Facial Protocol (Part 2)

Steps 6–10: Mask to Protect

6

MASK — Targeted skin treatment

Apply a mask suited to the client's skin type for 10–20 minutes.

"I've chosen a hydrating mask for your skin today. I'll leave it on for 15 minutes — just relax."
7

SERUM — Active ingredient delivery

Apply targeted serum after removing the mask. Press gently to absorb.

"I've removed the mask and I'm now applying a vitamin C serum to brighten your complexion."
"I'm pressing it gently so it absorbs deeply into the skin."
8

FACIAL MASSAGE — Stimulate & relax

Effleurage, petrissage and lymphatic drainage for 10–15 minutes.

"I'm moving on to the facial massage now. Let me know if the pressure is too much or too little."
"I'm using effleurage strokes to stimulate circulation and promote lymphatic drainage."
9

MOISTURISE — Seal in hydration

Apply moisturiser suited to the skin type to lock in all previous layers.

"I'm applying a rich moisturiser to seal in all the nutrients we've introduced to your skin today."
10

SPF — Daily sun protection

Always finish a daytime facial with SPF 30 or higher.

"I'm finishing with SPF 30 to protect everything we've worked on. Your skin has been exfoliated — it's extra sensitive to UV right now."
Language Focus 5 — Past & Future Tenses

Describing the Protocol: Then & Next

Facialists use past tenses to describe what has just been done, and future tenses to explain what comes next.

Past Simple — Completed Steps

Subject + past verb

Clearly finished actions during the session

✦ "I applied the toner a moment ago."
✦ "We completed the extraction step."
✦ "I used a glycolic acid exfoliant."

Present Perfect — Just Finished

have/has + past participle

Recent action, result still relevant NOW

✦ "I have just removed the mask."
✦ "I've applied the serum."
✦ "We have finished the massage."

Going to — Planned Next Steps

am/is/are going to + infinitive

Announcing the next step (planned)

✦ "I'm going to apply the serum now."
✦ "We're going to move on to massage."
✦ "I'm going to rinse off the exfoliant."

Will — Spontaneous / Reassurance

will + base verb

Promises, predictions, reassurance

✦ "I will be right back."
✦ "This will feel a little cool."
✦ "That will fade within an hour."

🕐 Protocol Timeline: going to → present continuous → present perfect (just) → past simple

Language Focus — Deep Dive

Giving Instructions with Warmth

In a spa, instructions must feel caring — not commanding. Compare these examples:

❌ Too Direct / Commanding

"Lie down."

"Don't move your head."

"Close your eyes."

"Stop talking."

"Take off your jewellery."

✓ Warm & Professional

"Go ahead and lie back comfortably."

"Please try to keep your head still — just like that."

"Feel free to close your eyes and relax."

"I'll be working quietly for the next few minutes."

"Would you mind removing your earrings?"

Softening Strategies

Add "please" + reason

"Please keep your eyes closed — I'm about to apply steam."
Command + reason = considerate

Use "go ahead and"

"Go ahead and remove your glasses."
"Go ahead and lie back."
Invites action without commanding

Use "feel free to"

"Feel free to let me know if you need a break."
"Feel free to ask questions anytime."
Gives agency to the client

Stage G — End of Service & Aftercare

Finishing the Facial & Aftercare Advice

Closing the Treatment

"We've finished the facial — take your time sitting up."
Allowing recovery time — present perfect
"Your skin looks absolutely radiant — the hydration really worked beautifully."
Positive closing comment — observation
"I'll give you a moment to get dressed and meet you at the front desk."
Next steps — future will
"How are you feeling after the treatment?"
Follow-up comfort check

Aftercare Instructions

"Please avoid wearing heavy makeup for the next 24 hours."
"Don't exfoliate at home for the next 5 days — we've already treated your skin."
"I strongly recommend applying SPF every morning, even on cloudy days."
"Stay out of direct sunlight for 48 hours — we used acids today."

Product Recommendations

"I'd suggest taking home our hydrating serum — it would complement what we did today."
Soft recommendation — I'd suggest + -ing
"Based on what I observed, your skin would respond well to a niacinamide toner at home."
"The cleanser we used today is available at reception if you'd like to take it home."

Specialist's Notes (for the ficha)

Written in past tense — record what was done:

"Double cleansed with micellar water + foaming gel. Applied 10% glycolic acid for 2 min. Steamed 7 min. Light extractions (nose area). Hydrating mask × 15 min. Effleurage + lymphatic drainage massage. Finished with HA serum + vitamin C + SPF 30."
